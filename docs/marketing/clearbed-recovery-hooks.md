# Clear Bed Recovery — Hook Library (views/engagement-validated)

Mined from the "Lead Generator" social dataset (145k videos, YouTube/TikTok/Instagram).
**Ranked by real performance — views and engagement rate (likes+comments+shares+saves ÷
views) — NOT the AI `hook_score`** (the score rated business one-liners highly that real
audiences barely engaged with; ignore it).

The source content is comedy / marriage / family / faith — not recovery — so nothing here is
copied verbatim. What transfers is the **format and emotional register** of what actually got
views, adapted to Clear Bed's audiences.

## The #1 finding from the real numbers

The hooks that won on views/engagement are **short, emotional, relatable, curiosity-driven, or
"personal-journey" updates** — *not* informational "tips." Examples that actually performed:
`"Is marriage hard?"` 35.5M / 12.5% eng · `"What you doing?"` 3.7M / 10% · `"I'm on the
struggle bus y'all"` 1.2M / 13% · `"This one is for you @…"` 17% · `"Tomorrow is the big day!"`
(Health/Personal-Journey) 15.4% · `"Goodness, you just ruined it"` 21%.

**Strategic implication for Clear Bed:** the highest-ROI play is a **documented recovery-journey
series** + **short emotional questions**, not explainer content. Build around that.

## Guardrails (non-negotiable for an addiction-treatment connector)

- No medical claims, cure/outcome guarantees, or "X days sober." We *connect people to care* —
  we don't treat. Keep that framing.
- Empathy and hope — never shame, fear-bait, or ask anyone to relive trauma specifics.
- Every published post carries the standing safety line: *"Not a crisis line — if you're in
  danger call 911, or call/text 988."*
- Run health creatives against TikTok/Meta policies (addiction/medical content is restricted);
  CTAs are "find care," never "buy."
- Audiences: `[PERSON]` struggling/considering · `[RELAPSE]` relapsed · `[FAMILY]` loved one ·
  `[HIGH-FUNCTION]` hides it well · `[FENCE]` scared of cost/judgment/treatment.

## Pattern 1 — Short, direct question (highest real performer)
*Real winners: "Is marriage hard?" 35.5M / 12.5% · "What you doing?" 3.7M / 10% · "Would you do
this?" 11.8% · "Agree or disagree??" · "What's your wife's favorite color?" 6.7%*
Why it wins: 2–6 words, instantly answerable in your head, drives comments. Keep them SHORT.

- Is sober scary? `[FENCE]`
- Do you remember last night? `[PERSON]`
- When did "a few" become every night? `[PERSON]`
- Are you the "fine" one? `[HIGH-FUNCTION]`
- Would you go if no one found out? `[FENCE]`
- How many "last times" is that now? `[RELAPSE]`
- Is it them — or is it you who needs the help tonight? `[FAMILY]`
- What are you really drinking at? `[PERSON]`
- Who would you call first? `[PERSON]`
- Tired yet? `[PERSON]`

## Pattern 2 — Open loop / "watch till the end"
*Real winners: "Watch til the end for an epic baby wipeout" 743k / 8.3% · "Watch till the end
for a surprise!" 9.9% · "a lot of people have been asking, so here's wha…" 558k / 13.8%*
Why it wins: an unresolved promise holds the view to completion (watch-time = reach).

- Stay till the end — the part nobody tells you about getting help. `[FENCE]`
- A lot of you asked how I actually made the call. Here's exactly what happened. `[PERSON]`
- Watch to the end if you've ever said "I'll stop tomorrow." `[PERSON]`
- The last 10 seconds are for the person who's scared to ask. `[FENCE]`
- Wait for what the intake person said when I finally called. `[PERSON]`

## Pattern 3 — Personal-journey / ongoing update series  ★ best fit for recovery
*Real winners: "Tomorrow is the big day!" (Health/Personal-Journey niche) 433k / **15.4%** ·
"Wife's surgery update" 327k / 8% · "Quick surgery update!" 6.5%*
Why it wins: episodic updates build a following that returns; "personal journey" is a proven
recovery-adjacent format. **This is the series to actually build.**

- Day 1. I finally asked for help. Following this all the way through. `[PERSON]`
- 30 days. Here's what changed and what didn't. `[PERSON]`
- Update: she said yes to treatment. Here's how the call went. `[FAMILY]`
- Big day tomorrow — first day of the program. Couldn't sleep. `[PERSON]`
- Relapse update — and why I'm not quitting on myself this time. `[RELAPSE]`
- Six months ago I made one phone call. Here's where it led. `[PERSON]`

## Pattern 4 — Relatable confession / "am I the only one"
*Real winners: "I'm on the struggle bus y'all 😂" 1.2M / **13%** · "How could she do this to me"
520k / 9%*
Why it wins: names a private feeling out loud; the right viewer feels *seen* and stays/comments.

- I'm on the struggle bus and I'm done pretending I'm not. `[PERSON]`
- Nobody knew. That was the loneliest part. `[HIGH-FUNCTION]`
- I'm so tired of being the strong one for everybody else. `[FAMILY]`
- Everyone thinks I have it together. I don't. `[HIGH-FUNCTION]`
- I keep promising myself I'll stop. Anyone else? `[PERSON]`

## Pattern 5 — Direct callout ("this one's for you")
*Real winner: "This one is for you @haleyshea.kelly" 537k / **17.3%** (top engagement in the set)*
Why it wins: speaking to ONE person makes the right viewer feel personally addressed.

- This one's for the person watching in their car before they go inside. `[PERSON]`
- For the mom who hasn't slept right in months waiting for a call. `[FAMILY]`
- If you've been to treatment before and it's scary to try again — this is for you. `[RELAPSE]`
- To the person who's "functioning" and falling apart quietly: you. `[HIGH-FUNCTION]`

## Pattern 6 — Curiosity cliffhanger statement
*Real winners: "Goodness, you just ruined it" 1.1M / **21%** · "Show me the lie here" 1.1M /
6.4% · "Simple answer to a simple question" 1.5M / 7.5%*
Why it wins: an incomplete or provocative line forces you to watch for context.

- The one thing the brochures never tell you about rehab. `[FENCE]`
- There's a free way to find an open bed tonight. Most people never hear about it. `[PERSON]`
- "Just go to rehab" is terrible advice. Here's what actually works. `[FAMILY]`
- Nobody warned me about the part after I said yes. `[PERSON]`

## Pattern 7 — Second-person scenario / role-play
*Real winner: "Quick scenario. Your husband just got home from the gym. What do you say?" 844k /
8.2%*
Why it wins: drops the viewer into a moment they have to mentally resolve.

- Scenario: it's Sunday night and you've promised Monday is the day — again. Now what? `[PERSON]`
- Scenario: you find it. Do you confront, or call someone who knows what to do? `[FAMILY]`
- Scenario: your kid says "I think I need help." You have 10 seconds. What do you say? `[FAMILY]`
- Scenario: you're winning at work and can't stop after work. Who do you even tell? `[HIGH-FUNCTION]`

## Pattern 8 — Vulnerable story tease (high views, longer form)
*Real winners (big views): "I just lost my job and I feel like I'm being stupid." 2.26M · "One
of the scariest conversations I was privy to…" 1.9M · "I got a lawyer letter…" 604k*
Why it wins: a real person + an inciting moment promises a full story (better for YouTube/longer).

- Three weeks ago a stranger answered the phone and didn't judge me. I haven't used since. `[PERSON]`
- Last March my daughter stopped answering my calls. Here's what I changed. `[FAMILY]`
- I'd been to treatment twice. The third time, one thing was different. `[RELAPSE]`
- The day a coworker quietly slid me a number changed everything. `[HIGH-FUNCTION]`

## Scale-to-1,000 templates
Each template × 5 audiences × ~3 situations × 2 platforms → 1,000+ variations. Keep guardrails.

1. `[SHORT EMOTIONAL QUESTION the target silently answers "yes" to]` (Pattern 1 — keep ≤6 words)
2. `Day [N]. [HONEST ONE-LINE UPDATE]. Following this all the way through.` (Pattern 3 — the series)
3. `Stay till the end — the part nobody tells you about [STEP].` (Pattern 2)
4. `I'm [RELATABLE STRUGGLE] and I'm done pretending I'm not.` (Pattern 4)
5. `This one's for [HYPER-SPECIFIC PERSON IN A SPECIFIC MOMENT].` (Pattern 5)
6. `The one thing [GATEKEEPER] never tells you about [TOPIC].` (Pattern 6)
7. `Scenario: [TENSE EVERYDAY MOMENT]. Now what?` (Pattern 7)
8. `[TIME AGO], [INCITING MOMENT]. Here's what happened.` (Pattern 8)

## Pull more raw material yourself (rank by performance, not score)
Dataset = "Lead Generator" Supabase project (`xjltsyviygqisleutgua`).
```sql
select hook_sentence, views,
  round((coalesce(likes,0)+coalesce(comments,0)+coalesce(shares,0)+coalesce(saves,0))::numeric
        / nullif(views,0) * 100, 2) as eng_pct,
  niche, platform
from videos
where hook_sentence is not null
  and length(trim(hook_sentence)) between 8 and 160
  and hook_sentence !~* 'bootcamp|chick.?fil|no hook|unable to determine|^pt\b|part \d'
  and views >= 100000
order by eng_pct desc, views desc      -- engagement-rate first; swap to `views desc` for reach
limit 500;
```
Only ~2,555 of 145k videos have a parsed `hook_sentence` today. The other ~78k transcripts can
be mined for first-line hooks as a separate batch job if you want a far bigger pool.
